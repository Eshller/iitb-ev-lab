Industry-Academia Participation Program (I-APP)

Industry-Academia Participation Program (I-APP) is a C1973 EV Power Train Lab association program designed to provide a platform for collaboration between the C1973 EV Power Train Lab at IIT Bombay and the industrial partners involved in electric vehicles and their components.

E-Postgraduate Diploma (EPGD) in E-Mobility

The program delivers a rich learning experience via a virtual classroom, featuring both foundational core courses and enrichment electives that students can choose to broaden their knowledge & interests.

Core Courses (2):

  • Introduction to E-Mobility
  • Battery technology for Electric Vehicle

Elective Courses (4):

  • Manufacturing Technologies for Electric Mobility
  • Power electronic converters for EV applications
  • Electrical Drives for Electric Vehicles
  • Electric Vehicle Grid Integration
  • E-Vehicle styling and design
  • (Additional courses yet to be included)

While both the core courses are compulsory and focus on power drive train & battery technology, the electives cover a wide range of topics related to electric vehicles.

Curriculum Focus:

Motors, Controllers & Chargers:

Electric vehicles (EVs) have become increasingly popular due to their environmental benefits and advancements in technology. Understanding these components is crucial for grasping how EVs operate and the advantages they offer over traditional internal combustion engine vehicles. As technology continues to evolve, the efficiency and performance of these systems are expected to improve further, making electric vehicles an increasingly viable option for consumers.

Battery Technology:

Battery technology is crucial for electric vehicles (EVs) as it directly impacts performance, range, charging speed, and overall user experience. Major highlights include energy density, charging speed, durability, lifespan, safety, weight, size, sustainability, cost & performance. The ongoing advancements in battery technology are pivotal for the growth and adoption of electric vehicles. As innovations continue to emerge, we can expect improved performance, greater affordability, and enhanced sustainability, making EVs an even more attractive option for consumers and helping to pave the way for a cleaner transportation future.

Manufacturing Technology:

Manufacturing technologies in production of EVs involves an overview of battery manufacturing, electric motor production, vehicle assembly, material technology & 3D printing, software & control systems and supply chain management. The manufacturing technologies behind electric vehicles are evolving rapidly, driven by the need for efficiency, sustainability, and performance. As these technologies advance, they will continue to play a critical role in making EVs more accessible and appealing to consumers, while also addressing environmental concerns associated with traditional vehicle manufacturing.

Grid Integration:

Grid integration for electric vehicles (EVs) is a crucial aspect of maximizing their benefits, both in terms of energy efficiency and sustainability. How EVs interact with the electrical grid depends on vehicle-to-grid technology, smart charging, energy infrastructure & storage solutions. By leveraging these technologies, then EVs can play a pivotal role in creating a more efficient, resilient, and sustainable energy system.

Styling & Designing:

The styling and design of electric vehicles play a pivotal role in their market acceptance and appeal. By combining aesthetics with functionality and sustainability, manufacturers can create compelling EVs that not only attract consumers but also contribute positively to environmental goals. As technology advances, the potential for innovative designs will continue to expand, shaping the future of transportation.
